Donating Blood...

...Saves Lives

Blood donation is a simple and safe way to help make a big difference in people's lives. Every two seconds, someone in the U.S. needs blood. Every blood donation can save more than one life. Donations are crucial for surgeries, cancer treatment, chronic illnesses, and traumatic injuries. By donating blood, you can help save lives and improve health outcomes for patients in need.

...Is Vital

Every human is different; their blood might be too. There are eight different blood types: A+, A-, B+, B-, AB+, AB-, O+, and O-. Blood type is determined by the presence or absence of certain antigens on the surface of your red blood cells. Universal red cell donors are those who have type O negative blood, while universal plasma donors have type AB blood. O negative blood is very rare, with only about 7% of the population having this blood type. While type AB is the rarest blood type, only about 4% of the population has it. This rarity makes donations from type O negative and type AB especially vital for patients in need.

...Comes in Many Types

Visiting a blood drive for the first time can be intimidating, but there are generally four different types of donations you can make: whole blood, power red (concentrated dose of red cells), platelet, or plasma. You can donate your blood at local blood drives, hospitals, donation centers, or at American Red Cross locations. Each different types of donations have different estimated collection times with most taking about one to two hours.
